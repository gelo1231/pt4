Dinakdakan

Knorr Pork Cube is used in Ilocano cuisine to make dinakdakan. Since one pork cube has the same flavor as one kilogram of pork, this small addition enhances the meat's flavor even further. With the exception of the pig parts, you probably already have most of the components for our recipe in your regular cupboard. So don't hold it against us if your family members start to obsess with Dinakdakan Ilocano!

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pig ears
  • 1 kg pig face (maskara)
  • 1/4 kg pig brain
  • 1 pc medium red onion, sliced
  • 6 pcs green chili pepper, sliced
  • 1 pc Knorr Pork Cube
  • 4 tbsp vinegar
  • to taste salt and pepper
  • 8 cups water

Instructions:

  1. step# 1 Pour 8 cups of water into a cooking pot. Let it boil, and then add salt and pepper. Add in the pig ears and face, as well as the Knorr Pork Cube.
  2. step# 2 Set the heat for the cooking pot to low and continue to boil for about 50 to 60 minutes or until the meat is fork-tender. Strain and set aside.
  3. step# 3 Boil the pig brains using a strainer and cook for about 10 minutes or until it turns white. Remove the cooked pig brains from the heat and set aside.
  4. step# 4 Heat the grill. Grill the pig ear and face for about 4 to 6 minutes per side. Wait until the pork turns a bit crispy, but not burnt. Heat the grill. Grill the pig ear and face for about 4 to 6 minutes per side. Wait until the pork turns a bit crispy, but not burnt.
  5. step# 5 Remove the grilled pig parts from the grill. Let it cool down a bit, and then chop into bite-sized pieces.
  6. step# 6 In a mixing bowl, combine the pig brains and vinegar. Season with salt and pepper. Add the chili, onion and salt, and then include the chopped pig parts in the mixture.
  7. step# 7 Transfer to a serving plate, and enjoy.
